A History of Oppression

In 1921, the neighborhood of Greenwood in Tulsa, known at the time as "Black Wall Street," was a center of entreprenurship and comerce in the south. In May of that year the entire neighborhoodwas destroyed by white rioters and resulted in as many as 300 deaths. This event was one of countless attacks made against black-owned businesses throughout history. (Image: Library of Congress.)
